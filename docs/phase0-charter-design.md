# Phase 0 — Project Charter & Success Definition
## Design Document: Charter Template, Metric Definitions, Decision Log, Risk Register

---

## Design Notes (how the research shaped this)

- **Charters are short and stable.** Best practice converges on a concise, high-level document (typically 3-5 pages) capturing objectives, scope, stakeholders, and requirements — deliberately abstract, with detail left to project plans. This charter is one document you can re-read in five minutes before any build session.
- **Success criteria must be quantifiable and pre-defined.** The charter should describe what success looks like rather than listing activities, using SMART-style measurable outcomes covering not just time/cost/scope but quality and business value. Here, every criterion has an exact formula and a scoring date (January), because vague criteria get retro-fitted to whatever happened.
- **The charter is the conflict-resolution and anti-scope-creep instrument.** When a mid-project dispute arises ("should we add weekly lineup advice?"), the answer isn't an argument — it's pointing at the scope section; anything outside becomes a formal change to the charter, not a silent expansion. For a solo project, the "dispute" is with your own August enthusiasm — the charter protects the critical path from it.
- **Assumptions, constraints, and risks belong up front** so the document gives a realistic picture of the project environment, reviewed again at close-out against original justification with lessons documented — which is exactly Phase 7's job, using this document as the yardstick.
- **Project-specific additions** (from the eval-first and pre-registration research in earlier phases): a *baseline bar* section — success is defined relative to what ADP/ECR achieve on their own, scored before any agent is built — and a decision log so every architectural choice is bisectable later.

## Appendix M — Binding Metric Definitions
*(These formulas are what Phase 2 implements. Defining them now, before any system exists, is the pre-registration that makes January scoring honest.)*

### SM1 — My Guys hit rate
- For each My Guy: record at freeze time his ADP-implied positional finish (e.g., drafted as WR18 → implied finish WR18).
- HIT = actual end-of-season positional finish (points per game basis, min. games threshold: __ games) is better than ADP-implied finish by ≥ 1 spot AND he returned startable value (finished inside 1.5x starter cutoff for the position).
- The dual condition prevents cheap hits (WR55 finishing WR51 is not why you reach in a draft).
- Hit rate = hits / list size. Target ≥ 60%. Also record: hit *for the pre-registered reason* (thesis validated) vs. hit by luck — scored separately in Phase 7.

### SM2 — Rank correlation vs. baselines
- Spearman rank correlation between each board (system / ADP / ECR, all frozen the same day) and actual end-of-season value rank (PPG basis, same games threshold).
- Player universe: top-N per position by consensus at freeze (N: QB 24, RB 48, WR 60, TE 24) — fixed universe prevents cherry-picking.
- Success = system correlation > ADP's AND > ECR's, overall and in ≥ 3 of 4 positions.

### SM3 — Tier accuracy
- A player scores if his actual positional finish lands within his assigned tier or one adjacent tier.
- Report per position; target ≥ 70% weighted by roster relevance (starters weighted 2x).

### SM4 — Process metrics
- Backtest gate: O3 passed and recorded before board freeze.
- Ops uptime: successful daily runs / scheduled runs ≥ 90%.
- Citation integrity: automated scan of final deliverables finds zero uncited quantitative claims.

### Baseline Bar exercise (do this in Phase 2, but define it now)
Score ADP-only and ECR-only boards from July 2024 and July 2025 frozen worlds against SM2/SM3. Record the numbers in the decision log. **These numbers are the definition of "the market" that §3 requires beating.** If the eventual system can't clear them retroactively, SM1's reaches don't happen — you draft off ADP and bank the learning.

## Appendix D — Decision Log (ADR-lite format)

One append-only file: `decisions.md`. Entries are never edited — superseded decisions get a new entry linking back. Every entry:

```
## D-### | DATE | TITLE
Status: ACCEPTED / SUPERSEDED-BY D-###
Context: <the situation forcing a choice, 1-3 sentences>
Decision: <what was chosen>
Alternatives rejected: <and the one-line reason>
Judged by: <which charter metric will prove this right/wrong>
Charter impact: <none, or section changed>
```

Seed entries to write on day one:
- D-001: Charter approved (metrics, scope, league settings frozen)
- D-002: Data sources selected (nflverse + Sleeper + FantasyPros) and canonical player-ID source chosen
- D-003: Backtest frozen-world dates chosen (July 2024, July 2025)
- D-004: Five-agent methodology split adopted (opportunity / efficiency / profile / market / context), subject to WBS 3.4 kill/merge criterion

## Appendix R — Risk Register (starter)

| ID | Risk | L×I | Mitigation | Trigger to act |
|----|------|-----|------------|----------------|
| R1 | Draft date arrives before pipeline validated | M×H | Phases 0-2 first; fallback = accuracy-weighted average (no debate); final fallback = ADP + changelog notes | M5 slips past [date] |
| R2 | Data source breaks / rate limits | M×H | Snapshot immutability means history is safe; second ADP source identified now: ______ | 2 consecutive failed snapshots |
| R3 | Hallucinated stats reach deliverables | M×H | Tool-only rule + citation scan (SM4) | Any uncited stat found in review |
| R4 | Agents converge; debate is theater | M×M | Falsifier-integrity audit (Phase 4 §5); gate cuts debate if it doesn't beat weighted average | Invalid-flip rate > __% |
| R5 | Backtest leakage inflates confidence | L×H | Frozen-world audit checklist; ADP snapshots dated | Any post-cutoff data found in a frozen world |
| R6 | Cost overrun | M×L | §7 ceiling, 75% alert, agenda/word caps | Alert fires |
| R7 | Enthusiasm-driven scope creep (in-season tools, extra leagues) | H×M | §4 out-of-scope list + change control via decision log | Any work not mapped to a WBS task |
| R8 | Small-sample overconfidence in January (5-10 My Guys) | H×M | Phase 7 scores process errors separately from outcome noise; multi-season commitment framing | — |

L×I = Likelihood × Impact (H/M/L). Review at every milestone; add rows via decision log.
